The story between the two

Guardian was born from a real frustration shared by eight designers and developers: design systems break not because people don't care, but because feedback arrives too late. We built a concept that intervenes at the moment of decision — not after handoff, not at code review, but while you're still designing.

After the hackathon, I couldn't stop thinking about it. The concept was strong but the prototype was rough — built under pressure, focused on demo not depth. So I kept going. I rebuilt the interaction model, added real pattern citations instead of generic warnings, gave it a visual language that matched my own portfolio, and added the features I'd been sketching in my head: apply fixes inline, push decisions to documentation, and a browseable pattern library that teaches while it audits.

The result is two prototypes that tell the same story at different stages of maturity. Guardian shows what's possible in 48 hours with the right team and a clear problem. Pattern Mentor shows what happens when you keep going — when you treat a hackathon not as an endpoint but as a starting line.
